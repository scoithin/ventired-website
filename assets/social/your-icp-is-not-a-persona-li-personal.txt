Your ICP and your buyer persona are not the same thing, and confusing them is expensive.

A persona is a person. Ahmed, forty six, Technical Procurement Manager, hates downtime more than he hates price.

An ICP is a company. Water treatment contractors, fifty to five hundred people, GCC, project driven revenue, buying through a nominated vendor list.

The persona shapes your opening line. The ICP tells you which two hundred companies out of eight thousand are worth calling at all.

One is a script. The other is a map.

I have watched three things go wrong when a team only has the script.

A market gets picked because someone went to the trade show, not because it contains a density of companies matching the profile. Saudi is enormous. That is not the same as Saudi holding four hundred buyers who look like your best account.

A distributor gets signed because they were enthusiastic and available. Two years later they hold the territory and sell to a customer base you never wanted.

And intelligence gets collected with nothing to measure it against, so the loudest opinion in the room decides what matters.

The fix is not a better persona. It is writing down four things: who to sell to, why they buy, when they buy, and who is still worth having in three years.

Define what good looks like. Then watch the market for it. In that order.
